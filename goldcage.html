Gold Cage

Gold Cage is a stats-management game in development about the price of maintaining power to protect the ones you love.

A duchess has long been pressured by the Queen to marry a noble man. There's one problem - she's in love with her female knight and childhood friend.

She has 3 days until she is officially married. Increasing her stats will convince the Queen that she doesn't need marriage to maintain her power as a woman, unlocking the choice to reject the engagement.

But what she doesn't know is that if she calls off the engagement, the noble man will kill her.

Process

Mechanics: Reach 5 of each stat - intelligence, charm, command, status, and wealth.

If goal is reached: The choice to refuse marriage will be unlocked, but that choice will lead to the Suitor killing the Player.

Mechanics: Each day, Player (playing as the duchess) has 5 actions per day to increase their stats, and different actions in Map increases different stats.
Per action: Stress +1 for each action that increases a stat, Stress -1 for each action taken to talk to other characters
At Stress 5: Player passes out, Player action count decreases by 1
Purpose: I wanted to show that attempting to maintain power through this stats-increasing is destructive, using the stress mechanic and the ultimate death of the protagonist when she chooses the choice that is unlocked by achieving certain stats.
